# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome! This `docs/` folder centralizes OctoAcme’s project management process documentation so new and existing team members can quickly understand how we plan, deliver, and improve work across the full project lifecycle.

OctoAcme uses a lightweight but structured operating model that starts with project initiation and moves through planning, execution, release, and retrospective learning. Teams begin by validating the business need, defining measurable goals, aligning stakeholders, and confirming a go/no-go decision before committing delivery capacity. This creates a shared foundation through artifacts like one-pagers, stakeholder maps, early timelines, and initial risk identification.

Once an initiative is approved, planning turns goals into an executable roadmap. Teams break work into shippable increments, prioritize backlog items with acceptance criteria, estimate effort, define a Definition of Done, and map dependencies, milestones, and release timing. During execution, delivery is tracked through clear workflow stages (Backlog, Ready, In Progress, In Review, QA, Done), with disciplined pull request practices, CI validation, and testing expectations that include unit, integration, and critical-flow smoke coverage.

Communication and risk management are treated as core delivery practices throughout the lifecycle. OctoAcme uses regular standups, weekly delivery syncs, milestone demos, and stakeholder updates, with a single source of truth for status and structured escalation paths from team triage to sponsor visibility when business impact is high. Risk registers, weekly status updates, and incident communication templates help teams maintain transparency and respond early to blockers.

Quality assurance and continuous improvement are built into both release and post-release workflows. Releases require acceptance criteria completion, merged and validated changes, security scans, release notes, rollback readiness, and post-deploy verification. After each sprint, release, or incident, teams run retrospectives, capture actionable improvements with owners and due dates, and track those actions in subsequent planning cycles to continuously raise delivery quality.
